The Apple 2

The Apple II was a great hit during its debut at the San Francisco's West Coast Computer Faire in April 1977. In the following year Apple put it in to production for the consumers and while the Apple II wasn't the cheapest personal computer on the market, it had a very strong following and support from hobbyists. With the great mind of Steve Wozniak, the amount of chips required in the apple II was greatly reduced. This was done by using each chip for multiple purposes. At the time this was completely new and thought of as pure genius.

When the apple II first shipped during June of 1977 it had some tough competition with RadioShack's TRS-80, because the TRS-80 was far cheaper than the Apple II. Although the one thing Apple had over its competitors wasn't, efficiency or price, but the idea of what a personal computer should be.

Apple, having progressed so far in just one year from the Apple I to the Apple II, gave others hope. It gave them a vision of how amazing these "micro" computers can become. With this start Apple grew, along with its products, and the Apple II became a part of a series of innovations.
